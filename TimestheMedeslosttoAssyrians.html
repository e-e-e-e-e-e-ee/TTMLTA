Times the Medes lost to Assyrians                                

 835 BCE | Campaigns in the Zagros (early Median-related regions) by Shalmaneser III
 Result: Assyrian armies campaign eastward into the Zagros and force local rulers/tribal groups in the region later associated with Media to submit and pay tribute.
Sources: Shalmaneser III Annals / Kurkh Monolith inscriptions

810–783 BCE | Eastern Zagros campaigns by Shamshi-Adad V and Adad-nirari III
 Result: Assyria conducts repeated campaigns into the Zagros, defeating local mountain rulers and reinforcing Assyrian dominance over regions bordering early Media.
Sources: Royal Inscriptions of Shamshi-Adad V and Adad-nirari III (Neo-Assyrian Annals tradition)

744–737 BCE | Campaigns against Median territories by Tiglath-Pileser III
 Result: Assyrian forces attack Median settlements, defeat local rulers, and impose Assyrian control and tribute obligations across parts of the western Zagros.
Sources: Tiglath-Pileser III Annals / Summary Inscriptions (RINAP 1)

716 BCE | Campaign against Media by Sargon II
 Result: Sargon II defeats Median rulers, captures fortified settlements, and reorganizes Median lands into Assyrian-controlled and tributary districts.
Sources: Sargon II Annals / Khorsabad Inscriptions (RINAP 2)

714 BCE | Eastern Zagros operations during Urartu campaign by Sargon II
 Result: While campaigning against Urartu, Sargon II passes through Mannaean and Median-adjacent territories, defeating local rulers and destroying resisting settlements in the region east of Lake Urmia.
Sources: Sargon II “8th Campaign” Annals (Khorsabad inscriptions / RINAP 2 corpus)

700 BCE | Zagros campaigns by Sennacherib
 Result: Assyria conducts military operations in the eastern Zagros, maintaining dominance over Median-related regions through raids, intimidation, and control of strategic routes.
Sources: Sennacherib Annals / Taylor Prism (RINAP 3)

681–678 BCE | Campaigns against Median border chiefs under Esarhaddon
 Result: Esarhaddon defeats Median border rulers and secures Assyrian control over eastern frontier regions, forcing local chiefs into submission.
Sources: Esarhaddon Royal Inscriptions (RINAP 4)

676 BCE | Campaign against Median rulers Eparna and Shidirparna by Esarhaddon
 Result: Esarhaddon defeats Median kings in the Zagros, forcing them to submit and deliver tribute to Assyria.
Sources: Esarhaddon Royal Inscriptions / Esarhaddon Prism (RINAP 4)

676 BCE | Median submission to Assyria under Esarhaddon
 Result: Multiple Median rulers formally acknowledge Assyrian supremacy and send tribute to Nineveh following Assyrian military pressure.
Sources: Esarhaddon Inscriptions listing Median rulers and tribute records (RINAP 4)

653 BCE | Death of Phraortes (king of the Medes)

Result: Phraortes is killed during conflict with Assyrian forces in the Zagros. The event is preserved in later Greek tradition (Herodotus) and is usually placed during the early reign of Ashurbanipal, though no surviving Assyrian inscription explicitly describes the battle or names Phraortes.

Sources: Herodotus, Histories 1; modern historical reconstruction based on Assyrian chronology
